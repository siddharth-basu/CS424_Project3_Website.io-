    • Data

    The data utilized was chosen by the professor of the course, Dr. Andy Johnson.

    For the application, we created bar charts and pie charts using the ggplot library once the data was processed into R Studio.

    Data Manipulation

  • The data was cleaned and reduced by python script given by Professor Johnson. This script allowed us to turn CSV files to TSV files, with various lookup codes added.
  • The attributes that a Tornado is classified with is magnitude, speed, time, length, which leads to some interesting findings
  • The attribute that makes a tornado destructive is classified by fatalities, injuries, cost loss, crop loss, etc.

  • Thought#1

  • When looking at the US heatmaps comparing arrivals and departures, the data is consistent on both sides and almost completely similar. We were very confused in this data, but then came to the conclusion that these are regular flyers who are also doing a return trip back to their state. We also took into consideration that people that buy airline tickets, typically also buy a return ticket. It is on very rare occasions, where someone will just buy a one way ticket.

    Thought#2

  • Another interesting data set we discovered was when we are doing a comparison of airline flights in a given month comparing with ORD and Midway. Looking at the airline: Atlantic Southwest Airlines only have flights at Midway half the year. We are baffled as a group where it is very strange that an airline is only active certain periods of the year.

  • Further diving into the ASA's revenue in 2017 peaked at 93 million, compared to United Airline which is a giant, their revenue neted around 37 billion. So we came to the conclusion that it is a much smaller airline company, with much less comercial planes than their competitors. However what threw us off further was seeing that ASA has a high volume of flights in O'hare. Also, Airlines like American or United dont even have flights in Midway. This means that ASA is not alone to blame for inconsistent flights in a year at Midway, but also because Midway is a much smaller airport, with less runways, and a combination of ASA's small stature and Midway's size are the main reason for this graph.
