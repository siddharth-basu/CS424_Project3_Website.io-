    Insights on How to Use

      Below is the menu system we have made to organize our tabs

    • The application is broken into 11 sub menu items with two helper items: About and Settings
    • You select on the item you want and have options on top of each page which render the page
    • You have the option to change the units (Mile/Kilometer) and time format (24 hour/12 hour)
    • Each menu item is unique and come with a variety of premises that you can choose from: Airport, Time, type of sort, delay, specific time, airline, etc.

    Example#1: Find all the given flights in a choosen date with delay data

  • This requires us to be able to filter through multiple days, we should click on explore dates
  • The top of the page, there is date tab in which you enter the date in 2017 (Calender style)

  • The date entered, 2 bar graphs and 2 heat maps should render which give hourly data of flights
  • The date is an hourly break down for the date specificed and be able to see both aiports.

    • Example#2: Find Arrivals and Departures for both airports (Hourly/Monthly).

  • First lets locate the Arrivals and Departures which will bring up to monthly updates
  • The top of the page, there is month tab which lets you choose from (1-12)
  • After choosing your specified month, the tables and bar graphs change to that data.

  • We have a side to side comparison with ORD and Midway which gives the user more options
  • Now we can see arrivals and departures for both Illinois airports with clarity (Hourly).

  • If we want to see a month to month comparison, there is a tab on top, which allows us to switch to month to month comparison in a heat map format to visualize.

    • Interesting Thoughts:

    • Thought#1

    • When looking at the US heatmaps comparing arrivals and departures, the data is consistent on both sides and almost completely similar. We were very confused in this data, but then came to the conclusion that these are regular flyers who are also doing a return trip back to their state. We also took into consideration that people that buy airline tickets, typically also buy a return ticket. It is on very rare occasions, where someone will just buy a one way ticket.

      Thought#2

    • Another interesting data set we discovered was when we are doing a comparison of airline flights in a given month comparing with ORD and Midway. Looking at the airline: Atlantic Southwest Airlines only have flights at Midway half the year. We are baffled as a group where it is very strange that an airline is only active certain periods of the year.

    • Further diving into the ASA's revenue in 2017 peaked at 93 million, compared to United Airline which is a giant, their revenue neted around 37 billion. So we came to the conclusion that it is a much smaller airline company, with much less comercial planes than their competitors. However what threw us off further was seeing that ASA has a high volume of flights in O'hare. Also, Airlines like American or United dont even have flights in Midway. This means that ASA is not alone to blame for inconsistent flights in a year at Midway, but also because Midway is a much smaller airport, with less runways, and a combination of ASA's small stature and Midway's size are the main reason for this graph.

      Thought#3

    • The image above is a representation of weather delays in the year 2017 from both Airports: O'hare and Midway. So we can visualize Illinois Airport system at a national scale. What baffled us here was seeing high weather delays in June, July, and August since Illinois has good weather in those month zones and we dont experience any severe rainy seasons as a state. So we looked at weather patterns around the country and came to the conclusion that this could relate hurricans in southeast of the country. The hurrican season in 2017 started in June 1st which supports this date set.
